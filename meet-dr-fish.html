Background

A concierge approach to physical therapy

Founder of P.E.A.K. Physical Therapy & Performance Training

P.E.A.K. was created by Dr. Kristan Fish as a concierge practice so that each client receives focused, one-on-one care instead of generic, high-volume treatment plans driven by insurance rules. Education & clinical training

Dr. Fish earned her Doctor of Physical Therapy degree from the University of Maryland Department of Physical Therapy and Rehabilitation Science, graduating with honors in 2008. She also completed her Bachelor of Science in Kinesiology at the University of Maryland College Park, graduating with high honors in 2005. Experience

Deep expertise in complex conditions

Neuroscience & acute care leadership

Before launching P.E.A.K., Dr. Fish served as the Neuroscience Team Coordinator at The Johns Hopkins Hospital in Baltimore, Maryland. In this role she evaluated and treated adults and children with neurological, orthopedic, cardiac, oncology, medical, and surgical conditions in a fast-paced acute care setting. [oai_citation:3‡peakphysicaltherapyandperformancetraining.com](https://peakphysicaltherapyandperformancetraining.com/meet-dr-fish?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

  • Helped design a Neurology Physical Therapy Residency in collaboration with the University of Delaware.
  • Trained staff on the JFK Coma Recovery Scale and developed tools to track progress in patients with brain injury and disorders of consciousness.
  • Served as therapy liaison to the Stroke Team, coordinating education and supporting quality initiatives for stroke care.
  • Participated in hospital-wide stroke screenings alongside a multidisciplinary team.
  • Mentored numerous student observers from physical therapy programs, helping develop the next generation of clinicians.

Academic & professional roles

Dr. Fish continues to stay closely connected to the profession through academic and mentoring work.
